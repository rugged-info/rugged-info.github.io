To All Employees,

As you may have heard, Mayor Caldwell has issued a “Stay-at-home” Order for Oahu residents effective tomorrow at 23 March at 4:30 p.m. Construction services are currently exempt from the order, so long as the services are provided using social distancing requirements described in the order.

We have asked our client to respond in writing that they would like us to continue work or if they choose, we will postpone their project until after the emergency. Currently, we have 3 projects that we expect will continue through the week.

Effective immediately, the following emergency work rules and procedures are to be implemented by all employees:

  1. Work in an isolated / separate part of the home from the clients whenever possible.

  2. Whenever possible, erect physical barriers, such as plastic sheeting, between the construction zone and the areas where clients are currently residing.

  3. If it is necessary to communicate with the client, maintain a distance of more than 6 feet. Don’t shake hands with others. Wash your hands A LOT, and cover your mouth when coughing or sneezing.

  4. Maintain a distance of more than 6 feet from your co-workers unless individual tasks require that you work in close proximity.

  5. When you are not working, strictly follow the Stay-at-Home order of the government. DO NOT endanger your fellow co-workers and those who could die from COVID-19 by refusing to take the Stay-at-Home order seriously.

  6. Wipe down surfaces within the work zone with disinfectant at the end of each work day.

  7. Minimize trips to the hardware store.

  8. Notify James or Bart IMMEDIATELY if you develop a fever, cough, or other flu-like symptoms.

Please know that, for your safety, we have asked all clients to follow guidelines for social distancing similar to what we have listed above.

Finally, if you have any questions or concerns about the safety of the work environment call James or Bart and we will do our best to get answers / solutions.
